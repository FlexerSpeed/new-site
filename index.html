Story of Odessa

The third largest city in Ukraine, Odessa is one of the most important port cities of the Black Sea. The city, which is also the largest port in Ukraine, stands out with its versatile industrial relations, trade activities and summer tourism. Odessa, which changed the face of the Black Sea during the Tsarist Russia, was also known as the fourth largest city of the 20th century Russian Empire after Moscow, St. Petersburg and Warsaw. With many opportunities such as museums, architectural buildings, theaters and universities, Odessa is one of the most known cultural centers of the country.
